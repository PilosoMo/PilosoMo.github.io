Data Center Outages & Fire Risk – Key Findings

• 57% of operators said their most recent major outage cost more than $100,000.
• 20% of operators said their most recent major outage cost over $1 million.
• Power remains the leading cause of impactful data center outages.
• Major fires at data centers have increased gradually in recent years, with lithium-ion UPS batteries being a contributing factor.
• Between 10–20 high-profile data center outages occur globally each year, resulting in significant financial losses and business disruption.

Power Infrastructure Risks

Recent Uptime Institute research found that power infrastructure is the dominant source of impactful outages.

Common causes include:
• UPS failures
• Transfer switch failures
• Generator failures
• Electrical distribution failures

These types of failures can lead to overheating, smoke generation, and, in some cases, fire—highlighting the importance of rapid detection and autonomous suppression systems.

Industrial & Manufacturing Fire Statistics

NFPA (2017–2021 Annual Average)

• U.S. fire departments responded to approximately 36,784 fires each year at industrial and manufacturing properties.
• These fires resulted annually in:
    - 22 civilian deaths
    - 211 civilian injuries
    - $1.5 billion in direct property damage

Fire Breakdown
• 25,021 outside or unclassified fires (68%)
• 8,077 structure fires (22%)
• 3,687 vehicle fires (10%)

Structure fires accounted for:
• 73% of civilian injuries
• 66% of all direct property damage

Leading Causes
• Equipment or heat source failures were among the leading causes of industrial structure fires.
• Electrical distribution, lighting, and power transfer equipment were the leading equipment involved in ignition within industrial facilities.
• Shop tools were the leading ignition source in manufacturing facilities.

Warehouse Fire Statistics (2020–2024 Annual Average)

NFPA

• 1,544 warehouse structure fires occur annually.
• Annual impact:
    - 2 civilian deaths
    - 17 civilian injuries
    - $314 million in direct property damage

Operational Findings

• Fires occurring between midnight and 6:00 AM accounted for only 18% of warehouse fires but caused 40% of total property damage.
• Operating equipment caused:
    - 43% of warehouse fires
    - 38% of injuries
    - 41% of property damage

Electrical Equipment

• Electrical distribution and lighting equipment caused:
    - 16% of warehouse fires
    - 18% of total property damage

Industrial Equipment

• Shop tools and industrial equipment caused:
    - 19% of warehouse fires
    - 23% of direct property damage

Material First Ignited

• Processed wood and paper products (cardboard, plywood, lumber, etc.) were first ignited in 41% of warehouse fires.

Why This Supports Autonomous Suppression

Many of the highest-loss fires begin with equipment failures that are difficult for humans to detect immediately:

• UPS failures
• Electrical distribution failures
• Motor overheating
• Bearings and rotating equipment
• Transfer switch failures
• Generator failures
• Industrial machinery
• Conveyor systems
• Shop equipment
• Arc faults
• Overloaded electrical circuits

These failures frequently begin with heat, smoke, or small flames before developing into catastrophic fires.

An autonomous vision system capable of detecting these early warning signs and activating suppression within seconds could significantly reduce property damage, equipment replacement costs, downtime, and business interruption.
