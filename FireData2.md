# Industrial & Manufacturing Fires

Source:
- NFPA — Fires in Industrial and Manufacturing Properties (2017–2021)

Supported statistics:

- 36,784 industrial/manufacturing fires annually
- 22 civilian deaths
- 211 civilian injuries
- $1.5 billion in direct property damage

Fire breakdown:

- 25,021 outside/unclassified fires
- 8,077 structure fires
- 3,687 vehicle fires

Structure fires account for:

- 73% of civilian injuries
- 66% of direct property damage

Leading ignition equipment:

- Electrical distribution equipment
- Lighting equipment
- Power transfer equipment
- Shop tools
- Heat-source failures

---

# Warehouse Fires

Source:
- NFPA — Warehouse Structure Fires (2020–2024 averages)

Supported statistics:

- 1,544 warehouse fires annually
- 2 civilian deaths
- 17 civilian injuries
- $314 million property damage

Additional findings:

- Midnight–6 AM fires represent 18% of incidents but 40% of property damage.
- Operating equipment causes:
  - 43% of fires
  - 38% of injuries
  - 41% of property damage
- Electrical distribution equipment:
  - 16% of fires
  - 18% of property damage
- Shop tools:
  - 19% of fires
  - 23% of property damage
- Processed wood and paper products are first ignited in 41% of warehouse fires.

# Messaging Guidance

Focus the mission page on these core themes:

1. Industrial fires continue to cause billions of dollars in losses every year.
2. Many catastrophic fires begin with electrical or equipment failures.
3. Power infrastructure remains one of the largest operational risks in mission-critical facilities.
4. Traditional detection often reacts after smoke or flames have already spread.
5. Autonomous computer vision can detect hazards earlier and activate suppression within seconds, reducing damage, downtime, and business interruption.

The tone should emphasize measurable risk reduction, operational resilience, and business continuity rather than fear-based messaging.
